Nes

The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) is an 8-bit third-generation home video game console produced by Nintendo. The NES featured a number of groundbreaking games, such as the platform game Super Mario Bros. and the action-adventure games The Legend of Zelda and Metroid. It was developed in 1982 and launched in 1983 in Japan and Europe in 1986. Following the North American video game crash of 1983, the Famicom was adapted into the NES which was brazenly launched in North America in 1985. It continued to aggressively compete with the next-generation 16-bit consoles including the 1988 Sega Genesis.

  • Life Span: 1985-1995 (10 years)
  • Introductory Price: $179.00
  • Units Sold: 61.91 Million
  • Discontinued: 1995
  • Best Selling Game: Super Mario Bros, 40.24 Million
  • CPU: Ricoh 2A03 8-bit processor (MOS Technology 6502 core)
  • Developer: Nintendo R&D2
  • Media: ROM cartridge
  • Storage: N/A
  • Controller: 2 controller ports
  • Sound:2A03 (NTSC 60Hz)

Snes

The Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES), commonly shortened to Super NES or Super Nintendo, is a 16-bit home video game console developed by Nintendo that was released in 1990 in Japan and 1992 in Europe. The console introduced advanced graphics and sound capabilities compared with other systems at the time. The SNES received largely positive reviews and was a global success, becoming the best-selling console of the 16-bit era after launching relatively late and facing intense competition from Sega's Genesis console in North America and Europe. It continues to be popular among collectors and retro gamers.

  • Year Realeased: 1990
  • Life Span: 1990-2003 (13 years)
  • Introductory Price: $199.00
  • Units Sold: 49.10
  • Discontinued: 2003
  • Best Selling Game: Super Mario World, 20.60 Million
  • CPU: Ricoh 5A22 @ 3.58 MHz
  • Processor: 16-bit Custom WDC 65C816 core
  • Media: ROM cartridge
  • Storage: N/A
  • Online Services: Satellaview (Japan only) XBAND (USA and Canada only)
  • Sound: Nintendo S-SMP

Nintendo-64

The N64 was first released on June 24, 1996 in Japan, and March 1, 1997 in Europe and Australia. It was the last major home console to use cartridges as its primary storage format until the Nintendo Switch in 2017. It competed primarily with the PlayStation and the Sega Saturn. Development of the console began in 1993 under the codename "Project Reality". he console takes its name from the 64-bit processing unit. It was launched with three games: Super Mario 64, Pilotwings 64 and Saikyō Habu Shōgi (exclusive to Japan). Time named it Machine of the Year in 1996. The Nintendo 64 was critically acclaimed upon release and remains one of the most recognized video game consoles.

  • Year Realeased: 1996
  • Life Span: 1994-2002 (8 years)
  • Introductory Price: £249.99
  • Units Sold: 32.93 Million
  • Discontinued: 30 April 2002
  • Best Selling Game: Super Mario 64, 11.62 Million Units
  • CPU: 64-bit NEC VR4300 @ 93.75 MHz
  • Memory: 4 MB Rambus RDRAM (8 MB with Expansion Pak)
  • Storage: 4–64 MB Game Pak
  • Sound: 16-bit, 48 or 44.1 kHz stereo
  • Controller: Nintendo 64 controller
  • Graphics: SGI RCP @ 62.5 MHz

game Cube

The Nintendo GameCube was released by Nintendo in Japan and North America in 2001 and in Europe in 2002.The GameCube competed with Sony's PlayStation 2 and Microsoft's Xbox. The GameCube is the first Nintendo console to use optical discs as its primary storage medium. The discs are in a miniDVD-based format, but the system was not designed to play full-sized DVDs or audio CDs unlike its competitors, and mainly focused on gaming instead. Reception of the GameCube was generally positive and the console was praised for its controller but was criticized for its exterior design and lack of features.

  • Year Realeased: 2001
  • Life Span: 1991-2007 (6 years)
  • Introductory Price: £249.99
  • Units Sold: 21.75 Million
  • Discontinued: 2007
  • Best Selling Game: Super Smash Bros, 7.09 Million Units
  • CPU: 32-bit IBM PowerPC 750CXe Gekko @ 485 MHz
  • Memory: 24 MB of 1T-SRAM @ 324MHz as system RAM
  • Storage: GameCube memory card
  • Sound: Analog stereo
  • Controller: GameCube controller, WaveBird, Game Boy Advance
  • Graphics: ATI Flipper GPU @ 162 MHz with 3MB embedded 1T-SRAM

WII

It was first released on November 19, 2006 in North America and in December 2006 for most other regions. Shigeru Miyamoto and Genyo Takeda led the console's development under the codename Revolution. The primary controller for the Wii is the Wii Remote, a wireless controller with both motion sensing and traditional controls which can be used as a pointing device towards the television screen or for gesture recognition. The Wii was the first Nintendo console to directly support Internet connectivity, supporting both online games and for digital distribution of games and media applications through the Wii Shop Channel. The Wii also supports wireless connectivity with the Nintendo DS handheld console for selected games.

  • Year Realeased: 19 November 2006
  • Life Span: 2006-2017 (11 years)
  • Introductory Price: €249.99
  • Units Sold: 101.62 Million
  • Discontinued: 2017
  • Best Selling Game: WII Sports, 82.90 Million Units
  • CPU: IBM PowerPC Broadway @ 729 MHz
  • Memory: 24 MB 1T-SRAM @ 324 MHz (2.7 GB/s) + 64 MB GDDR3 SDRAM
  • Storage: 512 MB NAND flash memory
  • Operating System: Wii system software
  • Controller: Wii Remote (Plus)
  • Graphics: ATI Flipper GPU @ 162 MHz with 3MB embedded 1T-SRAM

Wii U

Its was realeased in late 2012 and and competed with Microsoft's Xbox One and Sony's PlayStation 4. The Wii U is the first Nintendo console to support HD graphics. The system's primary controller is the Wii U GamePad, which features an embedded touchscreen, directional buttons, analog sticks, and action buttons. Response to the Wii U was mixed. It was praised for its innovative GamePad controller, improvements to online functionality over the Wii, backwards compatibility with Wii software and peripherals, and price; however, it was criticized for its user interface and functionality, and the GamePad's short battery life. It had low sales, primarily credited to a weak lineup of launch games, limited third-party support.

  • Year Realeased: 2012
  • Life Span: 2012-2017 (11 years)
  • Introductory Price: €299.99
  • Units Sold: 13.56 Million
  • Discontinued: 2017
  • Best Selling Game: Mario Kart 8, 8.45 Million Units
  • CPU: 1.24 GHz Tri-Core IBM PowerPC "Espresso"
  • Memory: 2 GB DDR3
  • Storage: Internal flash memory: 8 GB (Basic Set) / 32 GB (Deluxe Set)
  • Operating System: Wii U system software
  • Controller: Wii U GamePad, Wii U Pro Controller, Nintendo 3DS
  • Graphics: 550 MHz AMD Radeon-based "Latte"

Switch

It was released worldwide in most regions on March 3, 2017. The console itself is a tablet that can either be docked for use as a home console or used as a portable device, making it a hybrid console. Its wireless Joy-Con controllers, with standard buttons and directional analog sticks for user input, motion sensing, and tactile feedback, can attach to both sides of the console to support handheld-style play. The Switch competes with Microsoft's Xbox One and Sony's PlayStation 4. Nearly three million console units were shipped in its first month, exceeding Nintendo's initial projection of two million and within a year of release achieved over 14 million units sold worldwide.

  • Year Realeased: 3 March 2017
  • Life Span: 2017-Present
  • Introductory Price: €329.99
  • Units Sold: 84.59 Million (31 March 2020)
  • Discontinued: N/A
  • Best Selling Game: Mario Kart 8 Deluxe, 35.39 Million Units (March 21)
  • CPU: ARM 4 Cortex-A57 cores @ 1.02 GHz
  • Memory: 4 GB LPDDR4 @ 1331/1600 MHz
  • Storage: 32 GB eMMC
  • Operating System: Nintendo Switch system software
  • Controller: Joy-Con
  • Graphics: 256 Maxwell-based CUDA cores
