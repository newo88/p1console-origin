Playstation 1

Playstation One released in Japan in 1994 and Europe on the 9th September 1995 and was the first of the Playstation line of video games console. The Playstation was the brain child of Ken Kutaragi a Sony executive who managed one of the company's hardware divisions. He was later dubbed "The Father of Playstation". At the time of its release it primarily competed with the Nintendo 64 and the Sega Saturn. Fun fact. This wasn't Sony's first attempt at launching a video game console. Back in the early 1990's Sony and Nintendo teamed up to design a console called the "Super Nes CD-Rom" which later got cancelled.

Playstation 2

The Playstation 2 was first released in Japan on March 4, 2000 and Europe November 24, 2000. It is the successor to the original PlayStation, as well as the second installment in the PlayStation console line-up. At the time of its release it competed with Sega's Dreamcast, Nintendo's GameCube, and Microsoft's original Xbox. The PS2 offered backward-compatibility for its predecessor's DualShock controller, as well as its games. The PS2 is the best-selling video game console of all time, having sold over 155 million units worldwide. Sony later manufactured several smaller, lighter revisions of the console known as Slimline models in 2004.

  • Year Realeased: 2000
  • Life Span: 2000-2013 (13 years)
  • Introductory Price: £299.00
  • Units Sold: 155 Million Units
  • Discontinued: 4 Janurary 2013
  • Total Games released: 3,800
  • Best Selling Game: Gran Theft Auto San Andreas, 17.33 Million
  • CPU: MIPS R5900 Emotion Engine 294.912 MHz
  • Memory: 32 MB of RDRAM System RAM
  • Storage: Memory Card
  • Sound: PCM 2ch 48KHz, Dolby Digital 5.1 and DTS 5.1
  • Controller: DualShock 2, DualShock
  • Graphics: 150 MHz Graphics Synthesizer

Playstation 3

It was first released on November 11, 2006 in Japan and Europe and March 23, 2007 in Europe. The PlayStation 3 competed primarily against Microsoft's Xbox 360 and Nintendo's Wii as part of the seventh generation of video game consoles. It was the first console to use Blu-ray Disc as its primary storage medium. The console was also the first PlayStation to integrate social gaming services, including the PlayStation Network. During its early years, the system was negatively received, due to its high price €629.99. This was also the 1st playstation to have a wireless controller.

  • Life Span: 2007-2017 (13 years)

  • Discontinued: 29 May 2017
  • Total Games Sold: 595 Million
  • Best Selling Game: Gran Theft Auto V, 34 Million
  • CPU: 3.2 GHz Cell Broadband Engine with 1 PPE and 8 SPEs
  • Memory: 256 MB XDR DRAM system and 256 MB GDDR3 video
  • Storage: Removable 2.5-inch SATA hard drive
  • Sound: Audio output formats
  • Controller: Sixaxis, DualShock 3,
  • Graphics: 550 MHz Nvidia/SCEI RSX Reality Synthesizer

Playstation 4

It was launched on November 15, 2013, in North America, November 29, 2013 in Europe. At the time it was released it competed with Microsoft's Xbox One and Nintendo's Wii U and Switch. Built upon the x86-64 architecture, which can theoretically peak at 1.84 teraflops; AMD stated that it was the most powerful APU it had developed to date. The PlayStation 4 places an increased emphasis on social interaction and integration with other devices and services, including the ability to stream gameplay online or to friends, with them controlling gameplay remotely ("Share Play"). The console's controller was also redesigned and improved over the PlayStation 3, with improved buttons and analog sticks, and an integrated touchpad among other changes. PS4 became the second best-selling home game console of all time, behind the PlayStation 2

  • Life Span: 2013-Present (13 years)

  • Game of the Year: The Last of Us Part 2 (2020)

  • Memory: All models: 8 GB GDDR5 RAM
  • Storage: Capacities 500 GB, 1 TB, 2 TB[8]
  • Online Services: PlayStation Network, PlayStation Now
  • Controller: DualShock 4, PlayStation Move, PlayStation Vita
  • Graphics: Custom AMD GCN Radeon integrated into APU; clocked at 800MHz
