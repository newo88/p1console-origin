Xbox Original

The Xbox is the first installment in the Xbox series of video game consoles manufactured by Microsoft. It was released on November 15, 2001, in North America, followed by Australia, Europe and Japan in 2002. Its competitor were Sony's PlayStation 2 and Nintendo's GameCube. The Xbox was the first console to feature a built-in hard disk. The console also was built with direct support for broadband connectivity and with the release of Xbox Live, a fee-based online gaming service, a year after the console's launch, Microsoft gained an early foothold in online gaming he console, which had a manufacturing price far more expensive than its retail price, despite its popularity, losing over $4 billion during its market life.

  • Year Realeased: 2001
  • Life Span: 1991-2009 (8 years)
  • Introductory Price: €299.00
  • Units Sold: 24 Million
  • Discontinued: 2 March 2009
  • Best Selling Game: Halo 2, 8.46 Million Units
  • CPU: Custom 733 MHz Intel Pentium III
  • Memory: 64 MB of DDR SDRAM
  • Storage: 8 or 10 GB internal hard drive
  • Operating System: Microsoft operating system
  • Graphics: Nvidia GeForce 3-based NV2A GPU
  • Connectivity: 100 Mbit Ethernet

Xbox 360

The 360 was officially unveiled on MTV on May 12, 2005. It competed with Sony's PlayStation 3 and Nintendo's Wii as part of the seventh generation of video game consoles. Launched worldwide across 2005–2006, the Xbox 360 was initially in short supply in many regions, including North America and Europe. The earliest versions of the console suffered from a high failure rate, indicated by the so-called "Red Ring of Death". Several peripherals have been released, including wireless controllers, expanded hard drive storage, and the Kinect motion sensing camera. Xbox 360 is the sixth-highest-selling home video game console in history, and the highest-selling console made by an American company.

  • Year Realeased: 22 November 2005
  • Life Span: 1995-2016 (11 years)
  • Introductory Price: €399.00
  • Units Sold: 84 Million
  • Discontinued: 20 April 2016
  • Best Selling Game: Kinect Adventures, 24 Million Units
  • CPU: 3.2 GHz PowerPC Tri-Core Xenon
  • Memory: 512 MB of unified GDDR3 RAM
  • Storage: Detachable Hard Drives 20, 60, 120 or 250 GB
  • Operating System: Xbox 360 system software
  • Graphics: 500 MHz ATI/AMD Xenos
  • Online Service: Xbox Live

Xbox One

It was first released in North America, parts of Europe, Australia, and South America in November 2013. It is the first Xbox game console to be released in China, specifically in the Shanghai Free-Trade Zone. Microsoft marketed the device as an "all-in-one entertainment system", hence the name "Xbox One". It mainly competed against Sony's PlayStation 4 and Nintendo's Wii U and later the Switch. The console places an increased emphasis on cloud computing, as well as social networking features and the ability to record and share video clips. Games can also be played off-console via a local area network on supported Windows 10 devices. The Xbox One received positive reviews for its refined controller design, multimedia features, and voice navigation.

  • Year Realeased: 22 November 2013
  • Life Span: 2013-2020 (7 years)
  • Introductory Price: €499.00
  • Units Sold: 50 Million
  • Discontinued: 2020
  • Best Selling Game: Halo 5, 10+ Million Units
  • CPU: Custom 1.75 GHz AMD 8-core APU
  • Memory: 8 GB DDR3 (5 GB available to games).
  • Storage: 500 GB HDD, 1 TB HDD
  • Operating System: Xbox One system software
  • Graphics: AMD Radeon GCN
  • Online Service: Xbox Live, Xbox Game Pass

Xbox System X

It was released on November 10, 2020 as the fourth generation of the Xbox console family. The target for the high-end model to at least double the performance of the Xbox One X. The Xbox Series X has higher end hardware, and supports higher display resolutions (up to 8K resolution) along with higher frame rates and real-time ray tracing; it also has a high-speed solid-state drive to reduce loading times. The consoles is backwards compatible with many previous generation Xbox games, controllers, and accessories. The console are also compatible with the gaming subscription service Xbox Game Pass, as well as the cloud game-streaming platform Xbox Game Pass cloud gaming.
